CDMX - Istanbul, Turkey

Our journey to Istanbul began six months in advance when we found cheap RT flights using our Scott's Cheap Flights membership. We travelled in early May to celebrate our 12th anniversary and to take advantage of the spring weather. Remnants of COVID travelling hit us as immediately our flight to Houston was cancelled several times, making us miss our international KLM Royal Dutch route from Houston-Amsterdam-Istanbul. After several hours on the phone with United Airlines and KLM, we travelled to Houston, spent the night, and cut our Istanbul time a day short. After arriving in the evening, somewhat sleep, and some refreshing, we took "flight" into our Turkish adventures.

Walking Tours

Airbnb Experiences offers a wide arrange of tours to get situated in Istanbul. Here we are at an market, in an alley, full of amazing pottery. We made it to places like the Galata Tower, the Egyptian Bazaar, the Museum of Film, many alleys and stairs!

Baklava Galore!

Istanbul has dessert shops every 2 feet. Make sure to try one of the many varieties of baklava and Turkish Delights.

Al Pastor?

Sort of. Tacos Al Pastor are derived from Arabs arriving in Puebla and it's marinated pork. In Turkey, lamb reigns supreme. *Fun fact alert: Our guide told us the best Doner is in Germany.

Kebab

Our first diner was an amazing kabeb, served with about 10 different side dishes including baba ganoush, Turkish bread, roasted chiles and tomatoes.

Egyptian Bazaar

You can find tons of things here. Including an array of cheese shops.

Egyptian Bazaar

And pickles.

Alleyways near the Bazaar

You can find great eateries in alleys. Including a small hole-in-the-wall where we found "raw meat," a 3000 year old Turkish dish made with bulgur wheat, ground meat, tomato, pepper, pomegranate and spices. The paste is kneaded for an hour and although in the past we would have been with raw meat, currently that ingredient is left our for food safety.

Syrian Influence

Syrian migrants are having their moment in Istanbul. This soup is the doppelganger of Mexican barbacoa consome and we could not have been happier.

Lamb...oh delicious, lamb!

This eatery specializes in baby lamb shoulder kebabs. Dainty, perfectly seasoned, succulent little skewers of goodness!

Intestines, yes, please and thank you!

Adventerous eaters, we are! Love tripas tacos, so of course we'd have the lamb intestines wrapped around some meat wrapped with Turkish flatbread.

Turkish Churros

And yes, even in Istanbul we found churros!

Other Itinerary Items:

Clearly, these slides do not picture the complete list of places and eats we had over a week. Below is a list places and dishes to try:

# Name Location Notes
1 Kanafe Great dessert made with phyllo, rose water, pistachios, and a big hunk of Turkish ice cream.
2 Turkish Breakfast Tapas style and it comes with about 20 different mini dishes: eggs, cheese, honey, olives, tomatoes, bread and much more.
3 Simit Istiklal Street Think of it as a Turkish bagel.
4 Papatos Beyoglu Turkish baked potatoes are 3 meals in themselves. It's an experience.
5 MeatMoot Taksim Slow smoked lamb, served with small dishes, rice, bread. And honey over smoked lamb, yes!
6 Egyptian Bazaar Spices, cheese, pickles, meats, fruit, teas, jewelry. The bazaar is an experience.
7 Blue Mosque and Hagia Sofia Sultanahmet The grounds are beautiful, including an original Egyptian obelisk. If going to the mosque, remember to cover up.
8 Paragliding Airbnb experience: drive to the outskirts of Istanbul through rural roads, to the edge of the Black Sea and jump off to glide in the air.
